Why Daily Mini Golf Matters Right Now

When Putt.day hit the front page of Hacker News with 295 upvotes and 106 comments, the message was clear: people are hungry for a daily dose of mini golf. But the comments revealed something deeper — users wanted more holes, different themes, and the ability to create their own. The existing solution is a single, fixed course. That's like serving one flavor of ice cream and calling it a shop.

Current alternatives like Wordle or Lichess puzzles are either too abstract or too hardcore. Roblox is a full-blown platform that takes weeks to learn. What's missing is a micro-creation tool — something that turns a player into a creator in under five minutes. The signal from HN is not just "people like mini golf" — it's "people want to make mini golf, and they want it now."

With the rise of daily-game habits and the proven engagement of the "one-a-day" format, the timing is perfect. The community has already validated the demand. All that's missing is the tool. DailyHole fills that gap: a zero-friction, browser-based hole editor that turns your imagination into a shareable link. No sign-up required to start, no physics degree needed.

How It Works

1

Upload your background

Choose any 800x600 PNG image — a photo of your desk, a space scene, or a pixel-art castle. That's the canvas for your hole. The editor accepts standard image formats and gives you a live preview.

2

Place obstacles & the hole

Click to drop up to 5 obstacles (circles, rectangles) and set the cup location. Drag to reposition. The physics engine from Putt.day's public logic powers the ball movement — no coding required.

3

Share your unique link

Hit publish and get a shareable URL like dailyhole.app/play/abc123. Your entire level is encoded in the link — no database needed. Send it to friends, post on Reddit, or make it your daily challenge.
