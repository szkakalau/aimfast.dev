Why Hacker News Trends Matters Right Now

Every week, thousands of developers and technical leaders scan Hacker News to decide which frameworks to learn, which tools to adopt, and which bets to place. But the signal is buried in 18 years of unstructured comments. The original Show HN project — indexing 18 years of HN comments — received 637 upvotes and 143 comments in a single day, with users explicitly asking for keyword comparison, time-series analysis, and exportable reports. That’s not curiosity; that’s unmet demand.

Existing solutions fall short. HN’s native search (Algolia) is great for finding a specific post, but it can’t show you whether “Rust” has been rising or falling over the past three years. Google Trends is too broad — it mixes HN data with the entire web, diluting the signal from the exact community that matters most for tech decisions. Other tools like GummySearch focus on community monitoring, but they don’t give you a clear, data-backed answer to the single question every technical leader asks: “Is this technology gaining or losing momentum?”

Right now is the perfect moment. The HN discussion proved that developers are hungry for trend intelligence — they want to compare “Bun vs. Node.js” over time, they want to see the slope, not just the spike. With 18 years of historical data already indexed, the raw material is ready. What’s missing is a clean, decision-ready report that turns that data into a clear verdict. That’s exactly what we’re launching today.
