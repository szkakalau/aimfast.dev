Why Web Archiving Matters Right Now

Every day, valuable content disappears from the web — blogs shut down, pages get rewritten, and important references vanish behind broken links. A recent Hacker News thread about Kage (a CLI tool that shadows websites into single binaries) gathered 683 upvotes and 137 comments, revealing a deep, unmet need: people want to own the content they read, not just bookmark it. The discussion was filled with stories of lost research, vanished tutorials, and the frustration of relying on platforms that can delete content at any time.

Existing solutions fall short. Pocket and Instapaper reformat pages and keep them on their servers — you never truly possess the original. Browser extensions like SingleFile are powerful but require manual setup and technical know‑how. Archive.org is a public library, not a private vault. None of them give you a simple, one‑click way to download a faithful, self‑contained snapshot that you can store on your own hard drive, forever.

Now is the perfect moment. The surge in interest around offline‑first tools, the growing distrust of centralized platforms, and the increasing frequency of link rot have created a clear signal: people are ready to pay for digital ownership. SiteVault turns that signal into a service — no command line, no extensions, just a URL and a download.

How It Works

1

Paste any URL

Copy the link of the webpage you want to preserve — a long‑form article, a technical tutorial, a documentation page, or even your own blog. Paste it into the input field above. No registration, no browser extension, no friction.

2

We create a faithful snapshot

Our backend fetches the page and all its static assets — HTML, CSS, JavaScript, images — and bundles them into a single, self‑contained archive. The result preserves the original layout, styling, and interactivity, just as you saw it in your browser.

3

Download & own it forever

You receive a .zip file that you can store anywhere: your laptop, an external drive, or cloud storage. Open it anytime, offline, with any modern browser. No subscription required to keep it — it’s yours, permanently.

What You Get

One‑click archiving

No software to install, no command line to learn. Just paste a URL and hit enter. In seconds you get a complete offline copy of any public webpage, ready to download and keep.

Private & portable

Your archive is downloaded directly to your device — we don’t store it, and no one else can access it. Move it between computers, back it up, or share it with a friend. You are in full control.

Original fidelity

Unlike read‑later services that strip away formatting, SiteVault preserves the exact layout, fonts, images, and interactive elements. What you see online is what you get offline — pixel perfect.
