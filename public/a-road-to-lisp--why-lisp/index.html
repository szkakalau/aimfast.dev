Why Lisp Matters Right Now

Every few months, a Hacker News thread with 130+ comments reignites the same debate: “Lisp is beautiful, but I never got past the parentheses.” The signal is clear — developers are genuinely curious about Lisp’s power (macros, REPL-driven development, code-as-data), but they bounce off the steep onboarding. Existing resources like SICP or Practical Common Lisp are excellent, but they demand hours of reading before you write a single line. For a busy developer with 2–5 years of experience, that’s a non-starter.

Current solutions fall into two camps: either they’re too academic (SICP) or too sparse (Learn X in Y minutes). Neither bridges the gap between “why Lisp is cool” and “how to run my first Lisp program.” Meanwhile, the #1 question on r/lisp and r/functionalprogramming is “How do I start?” — a question that has no concise, actionable answer. That’s the gap we’re filling.

Now is the perfect moment. The recent HN thread scored 34 points with high emotional engagement but low actionability — meaning the audience is primed but stuck. With the rise of functional programming concepts in mainstream languages (closures in JS, pattern matching in Python), developers are hungry for the deeper paradigm shift that Lisp offers. A low-friction, 5-minute checklist turns that interest into momentum.
