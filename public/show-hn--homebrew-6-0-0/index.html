Why Homebrew 6 Compatibility Matters Right Now

Homebrew 6.0.0 dropped on June 11 with breaking changes that affect hundreds of formula. The HN thread (343 comments) is filled with developers reporting "brew upgrade broke my entire dev environment" and "I spent an afternoon manually testing each package." The official changelog lists deprecations, API shifts, and mandatory recompiles — but there's no dry-run that tells you which of your installed packages are affected.

Existing solutions fall short: brew upgrade --dry-run only shows what will be upgraded, not what will break. GitHub issues are a manual scavenger hunt. Reddit threads are full of post-mortems, not prevention. Developers are left with a painful choice: upgrade blind or spend hours researching each formula.

Right now is the perfect window. The upgrade wave is just starting — HN users are still hesitating, saying "I'm staying on 5.x until I know it's safe." The signal is clear: the community needs a pre-flight checker. BrewCheck fills that gap in under a minute.

How It Works

1

Paste your brew list

Run brew list --formula in your terminal and copy the output. Paste it into the BrewCheck input box — no signup, no login, no uploads.

2

Get your compatibility report

Our engine cross-references every formula against the Homebrew 6.0.0 changelog and GitHub issue tags. You'll see a risk score (0–100), a prioritized list of critical/warning/info items, and exactly what changed.

3

Follow the migration plan

Each flagged package includes a specific fix: recompile command, replacement formula, or config change. We also suggest an upgrade order — so you can migrate safely without breaking your daily work.
