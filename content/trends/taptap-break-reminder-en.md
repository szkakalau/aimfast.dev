## What is it

Taptap Break Reminder is a macOS app that uses the built-in trackpad haptic engine to gently nudge you when it’s time to step away from your screen. Instead of a loud alarm or a pop-up window that yanks you out of deep work, the trackpad delivers a subtle, physical tap. You feel it, but your visual flow stays uninterrupted. The idea is simple: break reminders that respect your focus. For indie developers who spend hours in code or design, this is a tiny but meaningful UX upgrade. It’s not a new category—break reminders have existed for years—but the delivery mechanism is fresh and hardware-native.

## Why now

Apple’s haptic trackpad has been standard on MacBooks since 2015, but most developers still rely on visual or audio alerts for productivity tools. Two shifts make Taptap Break Reminder timely. First, the rise of “calm technology” and flow-state productivity: users are actively rejecting interruptive notifications. Second, Apple’s recent API improvements make it easier for indie apps to access haptic feedback without deep system-level hacks. Developers are also more aware of repetitive strain injuries and burnout, so non-intrusive health nudges are in demand. The combination of hardware maturity and cultural shift toward gentle wellness tools creates a small but real window for this kind of product.
