## What is it

One-Person Business Templates are pre-built frameworks, codebases, and business blueprints designed for solo founders to launch micro-SaaS products, plugins, and developer tools quickly. Think of them as "starter kits" for side hustles — complete with billing, authentication, landing pages, and deployment scripts. These templates reduce the time from idea to first paying customer from months to days. They cover niches like subscription management for indie apps, boilerplate code for Chrome extensions, and even full-stack templates for API-based services. The trend is driven by developers who want to bypass repetitive setup work and focus on product-market fit, all while maintaining full ownership of their business.

## Why now

Several factors align to make this trend explode in 2026. First, the rise of AI code assistants means developers can customize templates faster than ever, lowering the barrier to entry. Second, the "creator economy" is maturing — developers see peers earning real revenue from small tools on Gumroad, Lemon Squeezy, and similar platforms. Third, major cloud providers like AWS and Vercel now offer generous free tiers, making it cheap to run a one-person SaaS. Finally, economic uncertainty pushes more developers to seek diversified income streams. The combination of accessible AI tools, proven monetization paths, and low infrastructure costs creates a perfect storm for template-driven solo businesses.

## Who's behind it

The movement is grassroots, not corporate-led. Key contributors include open-source developers on GitHub who share their own "first $1k" journeys, often accompanied by detailed template repos. Communities like w2solo (a forum for solo founders), devcommunity, and v2ex are hubs for sharing case studies and template recommendations. Notable individuals include indie hackers who publicly document their revenue — for example, developers who built $10k/month tools using minimal code. No single company dominates; instead, it's a decentralized network of solo devs who treat their own business templates as both products and teaching tools. This authenticity drives trust and rapid adoption.

## Related terms

**Micro-SaaS** — closely related, refers to small, niche SaaS products often run by one person. One-Person Business Templates are the enablers for Micro-SaaS. **Boilerplate Code** — the traditional term for reusable code starters; the new trend adds business logic (pricing, onboarding) to plain boilerplate. **Solo Founder Stack** — the collection of tools (Stripe, Supabase, Tailwind) that templates bundle. These terms overlap: a good One-Person Business Template essentially packages a Solo Founder Stack into a Micro-SaaS-ready product. Understanding these connections helps you position your offerings in the broader ecosystem.
