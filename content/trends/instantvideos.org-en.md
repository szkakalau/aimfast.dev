## What is it

InstantVideos.org is a website that serves up short documentaries, each roughly 30 seconds long. Think of it as a micro-documentary platform: you get a complete, bite-sized story or fact in half a minute. For indie developers, this is a new content format that sits between a TikTok clip and a traditional short film. It’s not user-generated chaos—it’s curated, concise, and designed for quick consumption. If you’ve ever wanted to learn a random fact or see a mini-doc during a coffee break, this is the product. The site is still nascent, with only one mention on Hacker News, but the concept hints at a shift toward ultra-short, high-value video content.

## Why now

The timing aligns with several converging trends. First, attention spans are shrinking—viewers increasingly prefer content under 60 seconds. Second, AI video generation tools have matured, making it cheaper and faster to produce polished short documentaries. Third, platforms like YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels have normalized vertical, short-form video, but they lack curated, documentary-style depth. InstantVideos.org fills that gap. Finally, the rise of AI-powered script writing and voiceover generation means a solo developer can now produce a 30-second doc with minimal budget. The market is ready for a format that delivers substance in a snackable package, and the tech stack to build it is finally accessible.
