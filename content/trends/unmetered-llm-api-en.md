## What is it

An unmetered LLM API is a service that gives you unlimited access to a large language model for a flat monthly fee, currently around $6. Instead of paying per token or per request—like you do with OpenAI or Anthropic—you pay once and use as much as you want. For indie developers, this flips the economics of building AI features. You no longer have to worry about runaway costs from heavy usage or unpredictable bills. It’s a simple, all-you-can-eat model for AI inference, ideal for prototyping, internal tools, or apps where you expect high volume but low margin per user.

## Why now

This is emerging because the cost of running LLM inference is dropping fast. Open-source models like Llama and Mistral have become efficient enough to run on affordable hardware, and providers are finding ways to aggregate demand and cut margins. At the same time, developers are frustrated with the complexity and unpredictability of per-token billing. A flat fee removes friction and encourages experimentation. The market is also seeing a shift toward simpler pricing in SaaS overall, and unmetered APIs are a natural next step for AI services.

## Who's behind it

The specific service mentioned here appeared on Hacker News, but the concept is broader. Small startups and niche hosting providers are the early movers, often running optimized open-source models on their own infrastructure. No major AI company has adopted this model yet—OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google still use per-token pricing. The community behind this is a mix of independent developers, cloud cost optimizers, and advocates for simpler AI pricing. They see unmetered access as a way to democratize AI experimentation.
