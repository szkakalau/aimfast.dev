## What is it

Homegames is an open-source game platform that one developer has been building for eight years. Think of it as a self-hosted alternative to services like Steam or Itch.io, but designed for indie creators who want full control over their game distribution. Instead of relying on a centralized store, Homegames lets developers run their own game server, manage user accounts, and handle purchases or downloads independently. It's still in an early, nascent stage, but the core idea is simple: give game makers a free, customizable foundation to launch and manage their own game ecosystems without paying platform fees or surrendering ownership.

## Why now

The timing makes sense. Over the past few years, indie developers have grown increasingly frustrated with the high revenue cuts and opaque algorithms of major app stores. Meanwhile, the open-source movement has matured, with better tooling and community support for self-hosted solutions. The rise of federated services like Mastodon and decentralized gaming platforms has also primed the audience to accept alternatives to walled gardens. Homegames emerges at a moment when developers are actively seeking ways to own their distribution channels, and users are more willing to try independent platforms that prioritize privacy and customization over convenience.
