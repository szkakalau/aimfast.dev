## What is it

LiveKit Realtime Voice Agents is a new package from Mastra that turns standard AI agents into voice-powered conversational interfaces. Instead of typing prompts and reading text responses, users can speak naturally and get spoken answers in real time. For indie developers, this means you can add voice interaction to your SaaS product without building complex speech infrastructure from scratch. Think of it as giving your AI agent ears and a mouth. The package handles audio streaming, speech recognition, and voice synthesis, so your agent can listen, think, and speak back with low latency. It’s designed for scenarios where typing feels clunky: customer support calls, hands-free operations, or accessibility features.

## Why now

Voice interfaces are hitting a tipping point. Speech recognition accuracy has crossed 95% for major languages, and voice synthesis now sounds natural enough for production use. Users expect conversational AI after ChatGPT and Alexa normalized speaking to machines. Meanwhile, indie developers lack the resources to build real-time voice pipelines — they need off-the-shelf solutions. Mastra’s timing aligns with the surge in agent-based architectures and the maturing of WebRTC for browser-based audio. The market is ready for voice-first SaaS, but the barrier to entry has been too high. LiveKit Realtime Voice Agents lowers that barrier now.

## Who's behind it

Mastra is the company behind this package. They are known for building developer tools that simplify AI agent orchestration. Mastra’s core team has experience in real-time communications and machine learning infrastructure. They previously contributed to WebRTC standards and open-source voice libraries. The LiveKit brand is separate — it’s a popular open-source platform for real-time audio/video — but Mastra has integrated LiveKit’s streaming capabilities into their agent framework. This collaboration brings together Mastra’s agent logic and LiveKit’s battle-tested voice transport layer. For indie developers, this means the package benefits from two established communities.
