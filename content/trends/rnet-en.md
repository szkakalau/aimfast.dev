## What is it

RNet is an AI credit wallet designed for everyday users. Think of it as a prepaid account that lets you purchase and manage access to various AI services without needing separate subscriptions or credit cards for each tool. Instead of paying per API call or managing multiple billing dashboards, you load credits into RNet and spend them across different AI platforms. For indie developers, this means a unified payment layer that could simplify how users buy access to your AI-powered features. It’s like a universal gift card for the AI economy, making it frictionless for non-technical users to try and pay for AI tools.

## Why now

The AI landscape is fragmenting fast. Every week, a new chatbot, image generator, or coding assistant launches, each with its own pricing model. Users are getting tired of managing five different subscriptions and remembering which card is linked where. RNet emerges at a moment when “AI fatigue” is real, and the market craves simplicity. The rise of consumer AI adoption, combined with the lack of a standard payment rail, creates a clear gap. Indie developers should pay attention because this signals a shift toward aggregated billing—a pattern that could reshape how small AI products monetize.
