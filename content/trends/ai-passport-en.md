## What is it

AI Passport is a new concept from Y Combinator-backed Egoist Machines. Think of it as a digital identity document for AI agents. Just as your physical passport proves who you are and what you're allowed to do, an AI Passport would verify an AI system's identity, capabilities, and permissions. For indie developers, this means a standardized way to prove that your AI tool can do what it claims, and that it's safe to use. It's still early days, but the core idea is simple: create trust between AI systems and the humans or services they interact with.

## Why now

We're seeing an explosion of AI agents and autonomous tools. As these systems start making API calls, handling user data, and executing tasks, the need for identity and capability verification becomes urgent. Without something like an AI Passport, how do you know if an AI agent is who it says it is? Recent high-profile incidents of AI misuse and the push for AI regulation are accelerating interest. Developers and platform owners want a reliable way to vet AI services before integrating them. The timing aligns with broader moves toward AI accountability and safety standards.

## Who's behind it

Egoist Machines, a Y Combinator-backed startup, is the primary entity. They've coined the term and are building the first implementation. Egoist Machines is positioning itself as the standard-setter in this nascent space.

## Related terms

AI agent authentication is a closely related trend. It focuses on verifying that an AI agent is not an imposter before granting access to systems. Another is AI capability attestation, which proves an AI model can meet specific performance or safety benchmarks. Finally, decentralized identity for AI is emerging, using blockchain to create tamper-proof identity records for autonomous systems. AI Passport sits at the intersection of these trends, aiming to combine identity and capability verification in one standard.
