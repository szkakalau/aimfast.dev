## What is it

WebDrop LAN Transfer is a browser-based file sharing tool that moves files between devices on the same local network without requiring any app installation. The sender opens a webpage, and the receiver just needs to visit a URL on their browser — no downloads, no accounts, no cloud uploads. It uses WebRTC or direct HTTP connections to keep data flowing within the local network, which means transfers are fast and private. For indie developers, this is the kind of frictionless utility that solves a real pain point: moving a document from your laptop to your phone or a colleague’s machine without emailing yourself or fumbling with USB cables. It’s pure LAN, pure browser, zero setup.

## Why now

Several factors align to make WebDrop LAN Transfer relevant today. First, remote and hybrid work is still the norm, and people frequently need to move files between personal and work devices on the same home or office network. Second, browser capabilities have matured — WebRTC and the File System Access API now make peer-to-peer transfers reliable without plugins. Third, users are increasingly privacy-conscious and tired of uploading sensitive files to cloud servers just to share them locally. Finally, the rise of multi-device households (phones, tablets, laptops) creates constant demand for quick, no-fuss transfers. The timing is right for a tool that cuts out the middleman entirely.
