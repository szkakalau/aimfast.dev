## 📝 Editor's Note

The aftershocks of Vercel Day are still echoing across Product Hunt, with a flood of "AI employees," "Agent workflows," and "custom models." Everyone's chasing tools to make Agents smarter. But the highest-scoring signal today is the least sexy thing imaginable: **DevCleaner** — a tool to clean up cache junk from dev tools and AI apps.

This reveals a counter-intuitive trend: while everyone's busy feeding data and installing skills into their Agents, the data being fed in is devouring hard drives at an alarming rate. **AI's byproducts (cache, logs, model files) are creating a new "digital waste" problem.** Who will pay first? Not the AI builders, but **every solo dev and small team whose hard drive has been bloated by AI tools.** Why this week? Because your drive is probably already full, and you don't even know what's taking up the space.

The truly buildable signal today isn't "how to make Agents smarter," but "how to manage the mess Agents leave behind." A $19 AI cache cleanup report, or a $9/month disk monitoring tool, is far more likely to get its first payment today than another "AI employee." The real grunt work is: figuring out exactly where each AI tool (Copilot, Cursor, Codex, Claude Desktop) hides its data.

## 🎯 Today's 2-Hour Build

### Product Name: CacheSweep (AI Cache Cleaner)

**One-Liner**: A Mac menu bar app that scans and cleans residual cache and model files from AI dev tools (Copilot, Cursor, Codex, Claude Desktop, etc.).

**Supporting Evidence**:
- **DevCleaner scored 111 upvotes and 18 comments on Product Hunt, earning a score of 36** — the highest-scoring signal today with cross-platform validation (2 platforms).
- This proves that "dev tool disk cleanup" is a real, widely validated need.

**Why Not the Other Two Directions**:
1.  **Goldfish (Personal AI Assistant)**: Despite 610 upvotes and 186 comments (massive hype), it only has data from 1 platform and sits in the "general AI assistant" red ocean — brutally competitive. A Builder can't differentiate a product in 2 hours.
2.  **MakersClaw (Slack AI Employee)**: Also high hype, but targets teams and enterprises with long sales cycles. An MVP built in 2 hours (e.g., a simple Slack Bot) can't compete with mature products and requires complex integrations and permissions.

**Pricing**:
- **$19**: One-time report — scans your Mac, generates an "AI Tool Waste Map," tells you which folders are safe to delete and how much space you'll reclaim.
- **$9/month**: Continuous monitoring — a menu bar icon shows current AI cache usage, auto-cleans weekly, with customizable rules.

**Fastest Validation Path (Doable Today)**:
1.  Open your Mac and manually check the sizes of these paths: `~/Library/Caches/com.github.Copilot`, `~/Library/Application Support/Cursor`, `~/Library/Application Support/Claude`, `~/.cache/huggingface`.
2.  Create a Google Form titled "How Much Hard Drive Space Are Your AI Tools Eating?" with 5 questions: Which AI tools do you use? How big is your /Users directory? When did you last clean up? Would you pay for automatic cleanup? Leave your email.
3.  Post on Hacker News (under "Who is hiring?" or relevant threads) and Reddit (r/MacOS, r/ExperiencedDevs): "AI tools are eating your hard drive: a quick survey." Don't pitch the product — just do the survey.
4.  **If within 24 hours, the survey collects 50+ valid emails and over 60% say they'd pay, this direction is worth pursuing.**

**Keep the MVP Manual**: The first version doesn't need any code. Use the Google Form to collect data, manually calculate each user's "waste map," and email them a Markdown report. This is a "white-glove service" — the cheapest way to validate willingness to pay.

## 🔍 Opportunity Discovery

### Solo-founder Product Launches

- **Daemons by Charlie Labs**: An AI Agent that auto-manages PRs, Issues, CI, and docs. Scored 30 points.
    - **Plain English**: Another "AI project manager." It targets the grunt work developers hate most.
    - **Key Judgment**: This direction is tempting but very heavy. It requires deep GitHub API integration, understanding complex project workflows, and has zero tolerance for error (an Agent messing up a PR is infuriating).
    - **Reverse Perspective**: If the AI Agent frequently messes up PR management (e.g., merging wrong code), users will abandon it immediately. This product needs extreme reliability, making trust hard to build early on.

- **Quartz: Local AI Email Client**: A Mac-native AI email client running locally. Scored 30 points.
    - **Plain English**: Emphasizes "local operation" and "focus," trying to balance AI privacy and efficiency.
    - **Key Judgment**: Email clients are the reddest of red oceans. Superhuman, Spark, etc., already dominate the premium market. Unless "local AI" offers a crushing experience advantage (e.g., offline processing, total privacy), it's hard to break out.
    - **Reverse Perspective**: Most people don't care if their email client runs locally; they only care if it's "fast" and "can help me write replies." Local models may lag behind cloud models in understanding and generation quality — a massive technical challenge.

### Surging Search Terms

### Fast-Growing GitHub Open-Source Projects

- **MemPalace/mempalace**: Claims to be the "best benchmarked open-source AI memory system." Got 55,848 stars.
    - **Plain English**: It solves the AI Agent's "goldfish memory" problem — letting Agents remember past conversations and tasks.
    - **Key Judgment**: AI memory is critical infrastructure. But MemPalace's star count is suspiciously high — be wary of marketing hype. Still, it confirms "Agent persistent memory" is one of the hottest tech directions right now.
    - **Reverse Perspective**: Memory systems have massive privacy and security issues. What if your Agent remembers a user's credit card info? Commercial products in this space may face strict legal compliance challenges.

- **the dotmack/claude-mem**: A tool providing persistent context (cross-session memory) for Claude Code. Scored 26 points.
    - **Plain English**: A plugin for Claude Code that lets it remember the context from your last session.
    - **Key Judgment**: This is more specific and practical than MemPalace. It directly solves a real pain point: having to re-explain your project context every time you start Claude Code.
    - **Reverse Perspective**: This feature will likely be integrated into Claude Code officially soon. As a third-party plugin, it risks being replaced by the official feature.

### What Developers Are Complaining About

- **"Reviews have become expensive, rewrites have become cheap"**: An HN post with 81 upvotes and 73 comments, discussing how code review is costly while AI rewriting is dirt cheap.
    - **Plain English**: Developers are finding it cheaper and faster to have AI rewrite code than to have a human review it. This is upending the traditional "write-review-rewrite" workflow.
    - **Key Judgment**: This is a profound industry insight. It means **code quality assurance (QA) is fundamentally changing.** Future QA tools won't be about "reviewing human-written code" but "auditing AI-generated code."
    - **Reverse Perspective**: Total reliance on AI rewriting leads to codebase bloat, architectural decay, and security vulnerabilities. "Cheap rewriting" ≠ "good rewriting." This pain point actually creates demand for "AI code audit" tools.
